# Research Question
> How steep is the socioeconomic gradient in high school distance running performance and depth, and how has it evolved since 2004?



 DESIGN A: SES Gradient in Track Performance (Descriptive + Structural Framing)

This is MUCH more feasible.

What you could do:
	1.	Scrape athletic.net
	2.	Aggregate to:
	•	school × year × event
	•	number of participants
	•	average time
	•	top-5 mean
	•	depth (number under a threshold)
	3.	Merge with:
	•	NCES school-level data (free lunch %, racial composition)
	•	District spending data (CCD)
	•	Census city-level income

Then estimate:
	•	SES gradient in performance
	•	SES gradient in participation depth
	•	Trends over time
	•	Heterogeneity by state

This is a serious paper.

It is not causal in the pay-to-play sense, but it is:
	•	Inequality
	•	Public goods
	•	Human capital pipeline
	•	Resource allocation

And it is publishable in spirit.

DESIGN B: Causal Pay-to-Play Shock

For this to work, you need:
	•	A state that introduced or removed fees statewide
OR
	•	A well-documented district-level reform

This is where your idea gets shaky.

Because without a centralized historical dataset of fees, you’re relying on:
	•	archived district websites
	•	local news
	•	FOIA requests

That is very high-risk for an undergraduate thesis.
