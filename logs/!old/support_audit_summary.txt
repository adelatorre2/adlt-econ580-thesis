Screening approach used
- Conservative token-based heuristic matching on active ingredient names.
- High-confidence controlled-substance tokens (opioids, stimulants, benzodiazepines, barbiturates, etc.) tagged as likely.
- Lower-confidence scheduled drugs tagged as possible.
- Cannabinoid-related or ambiguous terms tagged as unclear.
- No DEA schedule file or external authoritative source linked in this audit.

Key findings (exploratory)
- Likely + possible overlap: 2,081 of 27,413 FDA ORIG approvals (~7.6%).
- Overlap spans 1942–2026 with nonzero counts in 76 distinct years.
- Review priority within overlap: PRIORITY=40, STANDARD=666, MISSING=1,370 (substantial missingness).
- Orphan designation within overlap: 19 of 2,081 (~0.9%).
- Ingredient concentration: top 10 ingredients account for ~48% of ingredient mentions.

Biggest limitations
- Screening is heuristic; no verified DEA scheduling or ARCOS reference data used.
- Missing review-priority data limits priority vs standard comparisons.
- Active-ingredient text includes combination products, which complicates interpretation.

Recommendation
- Narrow. Proceed with a focused, likely-controlled subset (opioids/stimulants/benzodiazepines) and add DEA schedule/ARCOS reference data before any downstream-outcome design.
