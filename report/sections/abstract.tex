This paper examines whether faster regulatory approval of pharmaceuticals affects downstream drug distribution and diversion risk in U.S.\ drug markets. The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) operates several expedited review programs—such as Priority Review and Accelerated Approval—intended to bring therapies to market more quickly. While these pathways may generate substantial medical benefits, accelerated approval may also influence the timing and scale of drug availability, potentially shaping opportunities for diversion into illicit channels. I construct a dataset linking FDA approval classifications with measures of legal drug distribution and illicit drug presence using administrative data from the Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA). The analysis evaluates whether drugs approved under faster regulatory timelines exhibit different post-approval diffusion patterns and diversion signals relative to comparable pharmaceuticals.
