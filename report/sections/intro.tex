\section{Introduction}

I test whether faster FDA review classifications (Priority vs Standard, plus other expedited flags) predict faster post-approval growth in legal retail distribution (ARCOS) and stronger illicit-market presence signals (NFLIS), using a drug×state×time panel and event-time designs.

\textbf{Main question}:
Do drugs reviewed faster by the FDA (Priority vs Standard review classification) exhibit a faster post-approval growth in retail distribution—and stronger downstream diversion signals—than otherwise comparable drugs, as measured using ARCOS retail distribution and NFLIS drug-report indicators?
