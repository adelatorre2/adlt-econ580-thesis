

# Literature Review Roadmap
**Project:** Regulatory Speed and Diversion Risk in U.S. Drug Markets

This document is a step-by-step guide for conducting the literature review.
Follow each section sequentially. Do not skip ahead to data until Sections I–III are complete.

---

# OVERALL MASTER QUESTION

> Does faster FDA drug approval (e.g., Priority Review, Accelerated Approval) affect downstream legal distribution and diversion signals in illicit drug markets?

Your literature review must answer three structural questions:

1. What is the institutional setting?
2. What does economics already know?
3. What is the gap your paper fills?

# SECTION I — INSTITUTIONAL BACKGROUND

## I.A. FDA Regulatory Speed and Expedited Programs

### Motivating Question
What does “regulatory speed” actually mean in institutional and economic terms?

### Sub-Questions
- What is Priority Review?
- What is Accelerated Approval?
- What is Fast Track / Breakthrough Therapy?
- How has PDUFA changed review timelines?
- How are review classifications assigned?

- Google Scholar
- NBER
- FDA website (policy + historical pages)
- GAO reports
- Congressional Research Service (CRS)

### Core Keywords
- "FDA Priority Review economics"
- "Accelerated Approval outcomes"
- "PDUFA review time"
- "drug approval review duration"
- "FDA regulatory speed safety tradeoff"
- "expedited drug approval incentives"

### Keywords to Watch For
- “review classification”
- “regulatory lag”
- “post-market safety”
- “approval timing”
- “clinical evidence threshold”
- “endogeneity of expedited approval”

### Deliverable for This Section
Write a 1–2 page institutional summary:
- Define each expedited pathway.
- Explain how speed is measured.
- Explain why speed might matter economically.
